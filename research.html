Flexible spatial reconstruction of single-cell gene expression

novoSpaRc is a computational framework that probabilistically assigns cells to tissue locations. At the core of this framework lies a structural correspondence hypothesis, that cells in physical proximity share similar gene expression profiles. Given scRNA-seq data, novoSpaRc spatially reconstructs tissues based on this hypothesis, and optionally, by including a reference atlas of marker genes to improve reconstruction. [24]

Gene expression cartography

Reconstruct spatial positions with little or no prior knowledge, by searching for spatial arrangements of sequenced cells in which nearby cells have transcriptional profiles that are often more similar than cells that are further apart. We formulate this task as a generalized optimal-transport problem for probabilistic embedding and derive an efficient iterative algorithm to solve it. [18]

FLAM-seq: full-length mRNA sequencing

Although messenger RNAs are key molecules for understanding life, until now, no method has existed to determine the full-length sequence of endogenous mRNAs including their poly(A) tails. Moreover, although non-A nucleotides can be incorporated in poly(A) tails, there also exists no method to accurately sequence them. Here, we present full-length poly(A) and mRNA sequencing (FLAM-seq), a rapid and simple method for high-quality sequencing of entire mRNAs. We report a complementary DNA library preparation method coupled to single-molecule sequencing to perform FLAM-seq. Using human cell lines, brain organoids and Caenorhabditis elegans we show that FLAM-seq delivers high-quality full-length mRNA sequences for thousands of different genes per sample.[17]

A Single-cell transcriptome atlas of the mouse glomerulus

Our study comprehensively characterized gene expression in individual glomerular cells and sets the stage for the dissection of glomerular function at the single-cell level in health and disease [16].

The virtual fly embryo

The common fruitfly has many similarities with the human species. This unexpected fact has helped us to understand basic genetic principles and identify disease genes. I used single cell RNA data and machine learning algorithms to create a virtual Drosophila early embryo that can be queried for the expression of any given gene [15]. I created an interactive online tool that provides the transcriptional information along the virtual embryo and can be used as a resource: DVEX.

Fixing and preserving precious RNA material in single cells

It is now possible to quickly obtain the RNA of thousands of single cells at low cost. As it is critical to preserve the natural cell states, we developed a cell fixation method which stabilises the cells and preserves them over time [14]. In close communication with the experimentalists, I performed the whole computational and statistical analysis of the data.

Topological quanum computer elements

Functional quantum computers will be able to perform computations at ultra high speed and accuracy. A topological quantum computer is based on exotic quasi-particles called anyons, a family of which has been though experimentally detected. My research in this field focused on extracting mathematical structures that allowed us to solve the corresponding systems exactly [10] and [13].

Interacting fermions: a · b = - b · a

Fermions are a large class of particles which obey the exclusion principle discovered by Pauli. Most fundamental blocks of matter are fermions, namely all quarks and leptons, such as the electron. The interactions between fermions are non-trivial, especially because of their intrinsic properties. We solved a model of a 1-dimensional lattice of interacting fermions subject to boundary conditions [8]. I then generalized the boundary conditions to a large unstudied family of supersymmetric models [9].

Impurities, imperfections, defects

Another route to bridge the gap between theory and nature is to consider impurities. Quite often, the theoretical models are far too homogeneous to capture physical signals. Since nature is almost always not homogeneous, or perfect, these impurities are also called imperfections, or defects. I was excited to work out the consequences of introducing such impurities in diverse frameworks including sigma models [6], spin chain impurities [7] and impurity generation boundaries [11], [12].

Life in 10 dimensions

String theory started as a promising attempt to unify all physical interactions and ended up being a vast compilation of mathematical theories and physical concepts. According to string theory, our world is 10-dimensional. Peculiar objects roam on those high-dimensional spaces and interact in even more peculiar manners with each other. Fascinated by the beauty of the mathematical structures, I worked on finding new ways to embed those objects on sphere submanifolds [4].

Boundary conditions: when theory moves towards reality

Many theoretical physics models are solvable because they are unrealistic. We can extract, however, fruitful insights and qualitative information by studying these models. I got early on interested in finding ways to bridging that gap. In spin chains, one way forward is to introduce boundary conditions so that the particles reflect on the edges. The system is no longer infinite, so that finite size effects become important. This work resulted in three publications [1], [2], [5] and a review article [3].
