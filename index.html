I have a solid quantitative training in natural sciences, obtained my PhD in 2012 and have been wokring in research since. My education was primarily on theoretical physics, but apart from experience in mathematics and statistics, I've been developing data analysis and software development skills.

In 2015 I transited from theoretical physics to computational biology, and I'm currently working in the Max Delbrück Center for Molecular Medicine in Berlin. My work involves data preparation and analysis of high-throughput sequencing data, as well as performing statistical and computational modelling.

Below are a few highlighted projects I've completed. Detailed descriptions can be found here.

  • DVEX, a 3D interactive virtual fly embryo built in R and Shiny.
  • Dropbead, an R package for data analysis of single-cell sequencing data.
  • A text prediction application coded in Python and R.

I love learning and a portion of my free time is spent on participating in machine learning competitions or coding small projects. Examples include

  • The Outbrain Kaggle machine learning competition, where I ranked 25th across 979 teams. Source code.
  • A mileage calculator, written in Python.
